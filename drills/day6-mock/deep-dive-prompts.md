# Day 6: Mock Deep Dive — Answer These ALOUD

## Instructions
1. Set a timer for each question: 3-5 minutes max
2. Stand up if possible (simulates onsite energy)
3. Answer as if Andy Carroll is sitting across from you
4. Record yourself on your phone — listen back for filler words, vagueness, or rambling
5. After each answer, note: what did I miss? What was unclear?

---

## Questions

### 1. "Walk me through the most complex infrastructure you've built end-to-end."
*Timer: 5 minutes. Draw on paper as you talk.*

### 2. "You mentioned air-gap experience. How did you actually get software from the internet side to the disconnected side? Walk me through every step."
*Timer: 4 minutes. Be specific — tools, formats, validation.*

### 3. "Tell me about your Ansible experience. Are you writing your own roles or mostly integrating existing ones?"
*Timer: 3 minutes. Give specific examples: what modules, what patterns, custom vs. community.*

### 4. "Have you stood up RKE2 clusters from scratch? Walk me through the bootstrap process."
*Timer: 4 minutes. Be specific: node prep, binary install, config, CNI, air-gap considerations.*

### 5. "Walk me through what happens at every network layer when a pod in one cluster calls an external API."
*Timer: 4 minutes. Trace every hop: pod → CNI → node → VPC → NAT → firewall → internet.*

### 6. "Tell me about a time something broke in production. What happened and how did you fix it?"
*Timer: 3 minutes. Use Story 14 (Kapitan) or a production incident. Own it, don't minimize.*

### 7. "How would you design a system to populate container registries on air-gapped networks using a diode?"
*Timer: 5 minutes. This is THE Anduril question. Draw as you explain.*

### 8. "Why do you want to work at Anduril? Why defense tech?"
*Timer: 2 minutes. Be genuine. Reference your DoD background and what excites you.*

### 9. "You'd be coming in as engineer number three on a growing team. How do you approach joining a team that's already moving fast?"
*Timer: 3 minutes. Show: listen first, find quick wins, earn trust, then propose bigger changes.*

### 10. "What's one thing you'd do differently if you could re-architect a system you built?"
*Timer: 3 minutes. Use Story 19 (CaC/IaC coupling). Show self-awareness.*
